ON THE SPECTRALITY OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

In art, images, text and society; what are people afraid of?

art
machine learning
Author

Tom Savage

Published

January 3, 2024

AI takes language, images, data and, through the use of latent variables places these ideas within space.

Through tokenisation: images, language, and art is given space. Conceptually is taken from the aether and into an (albiet high-dimensional) space.

In the context of electronic life, Art was produced by participants and embedding was used. Large language models used to correlate the prompts etc etc …. use of embedding and using AI to infer the relations beteen things, underlying world model.

In this moment these ideas enter the spectral domain.

Mathematical operations then move and shift this space to fit these ideas, and out the other side other language comes out of the other side.

Despite being of the same form as the inputs, these outputs come back ‘with something’, somewhat tainted … why?

These tainted outputs, language and images are easy to spot for most people, and can be considered unnerving, or unnatural.

Not only are the outputs themselves are uncanny, but they are made uncanny regardless by entering and exiting this mathematical space. By exiting the aether, entering space, and then reenterig the aether. Having been rigorourly mathematically defined, transformed, then undefined they return into the conversation somewhat different.

Generative art is very prevelant but it has diminished impact in other circles. Art produced by code can be interpreted similarly as AI producing things.

The code itself exists as an inbetween for ideas, concepts have been laundered in a very rigid space, and out the other side they come out somewhat tainted.

Is there an example of another medium that has been shunned in this sense throughout history? How did it get overcome, what barriers were there for people who didn’t get it?

This may explain the lack of prevelance of coded art and code as a medium, the sense of impersonality and the separation between the art and the artist as code is an intermediary.

In electronic life, by giving ideas a space they become uncanny. By showing people how AI works.

By Mapping human ideas created in isolation, they become spectral and uncanny, as they are presented and made to exist within a digital space (vector space) which has spectral properties over concepts just in the aether.

There is a relationship here between collected ideas and group conciousness.

Can the US be concious? With embedding and AI, placing ideas in space is like this.

Is the presentation of ideas and the fact they’re all linked what makes it uncanny? The spectral spaces of artificial intelligence.

The idea that people are a part of a group conciousness alongside a personal conciousness.

By peeling back the curtain, some people reject this. They are shown that you are part of a group conciousness and AI allows us to interact with this directly. AI has also itself been created by the group conciousness!

This could be why AI and art is shunned, the idea that you exist and your ideas exist within a wider spectral space of group collective conciousness. And AI forces us to reason with this fact, that we are spectral and our ideas exist whtin this spectral space.

the uncanny-ness we feel is a feeling towards ourselves and our group collective conciousness.

People don’t like their ideas existing within a wider space. They can a spectrality that forces them to acknowledge their place within a potential wider conciousness.

Where does AI and art fit within all this?

There is an arguement that AI models themselves not their inputs or outputs are spectral. They provide space to the ideas and it is the model itself, the transformation from a normal piece of text to a space, that is unnerving.

You have a piece of text entering this digital space, but the process that does this is AI. The AI therefore isn’t the spectral space itself that the idea enters, but it is the enabler.

AI itself forces people to acknowledge the existence of this space of ideas and… therefore this group collective conciousness, which individual conciousnesses woud reject.

AI models are the transformation into this space, the messengers, the transporters, they have a friendly relationship with the space itself and ezist alongside but they are the portal.

It is the interaction with AI that is the interesting thing. How people face and interact with the ability to enter a spectral space. And how the ideas that exist the space are interpreted.

When people are afraid of AI are they afraid of the consequences or are they afraid of the AI taking actions (performing transformations) to enable these consequences?

Are people afraid that these consequences are born of the spectral domain?

Are we afraid of this presence that is pulling strings within the spectral space of ideas? That has given ideas a physicality. AI has the ability to bring ideas and concepts into a physical space.

Lots of this applies only to embeddings like text embeddings, but in the case of feed forward neurals, it is the neural network itself that is this space.

The idea that an outcome or impact can be felt as a result of something being taken from something as fluid and creative as images, and art… go through a rigid and well defined procedure and come out as an image or language… the network has a haunting to it wherein we don’t trust the outcomes as much, especially because sometimes we can see errors.

6 fingers for example are literally like an alien, but also remind us of a lack of understanding.

Derrida 1994: …capable beyond the opposition between the presence and the impresence, actuality and non actuality, life and nonlife, and thinking of the positioning of the spector.

AI as a conceptual spector, allowing ideas to enter a spectral space and then letting them leave.

Electronic life forced people to face this spectral presence.

Secular horror offers a glimpse of the absolute black nothingness that lies between the maze of appearences. A revelation that there is no transecendant reality, onlt the final death of meaning.

Our group collective conciousness that has created both AI methods and AI models…the result is these methodologies not having any actual understanding. It is the final death of meaning, the realisation that us and only us have created this spector.

What other art has forced people to reason this way?

  • Individuals created AI (groups and collectives)
  • AI models and AI methods have both been defined by us.

Spectral determinism, to have never been a subject in own right.

What adds an additional dimension is that we set the rules. AI has been defined and created by us. The ability of us to define a spectral space could lead to spectral determinsism via AI. Or collective conciousness determinsism?

We create something, we create the data, and get it doesn’t feel right, the outputs have interfaced with our group collective conciousness?

We create AI through years of research, AI presents ideas from ‘us’, individuals faces with both these facts.

  1. We created a spector and the spectral space
  2. We exist within that spectral space
